Design Philosophy

I believe design should be clear and simple. I begin by asking what the user needs to do, and I make small tests to check ideas. Sometimes I use hardware like Arduino, sometimes I use a quick web demo. Each step is written down so it can be explained later. I think about access for everyone, using clear text, enough contrast, and easy navigation. I test often in small ways and improve as I go. I prefer to build in small parts, show progress early, and make changes easy when feedback comes in.

Case Study — Making Data Use Visible

Context

People rarely know what happens to their content once it’s shared. My role was to translate privacy language into an interface that feels direct, visual, and easy to act on.

Approach

  • Designed mobile-first screens that stay readable on small devices.
  • Kept interactions lightweight, fast, and accessible without extra libraries.
  • Mapped a simple story: Collect → Process → Control.

Design Moves

  • Used progressive disclosure to reduce overload.
  • High-contrast typography and direct labels instead of abstract icons.
  • No hidden or sticky UI that blocks screen readers.

Validation

  • Test prompt: “Show me what happens to your story after you post.”
  • Clearer answers from users, fewer wrong taps, more confidence in flow.

Impact & Next Steps

  • Improved comprehension of data movement inside the app.
  • Future additions: a plain-language glossary and a “download my data” path.
